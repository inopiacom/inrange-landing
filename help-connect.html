Helping connect a Dexcom sensor

A young person you care about is using InRange to help manage their diabetes and asked for help connecting their glucose sensor. It takes about two minutes, and you do it together on their phone — you never have to share a password over text.

What you'll need

  • Their phone, with the InRange app open on the sensor step.
  • The Dexcom (or Dexcom Share) login — username and password.
  • Dexcom "Share" turned on in the Dexcom app (so readings can flow through).

Steps

  1. On their phone, in InRange, tap “I have my own Dexcom login” on the sensor step.
  2. Choose Dexcom and the correct region (US or Outside US).
  3. Enter the Dexcom username and password. It's stored securely in the phone's Keychain — only on that device.
  4. Tap connect. Within a few minutes their glucose graph lights up.

Prefer Apple Health? If glucose is already shared to Apple Health (for example via family sharing), you can instead tap “Connect Apple Health” — often the simplest route.

Your privacy

InRange is a wellness app, not a medical device. Login details are kept only in the device's secure Keychain — InRange's servers never store the Dexcom password. See our Privacy Policy for details.
